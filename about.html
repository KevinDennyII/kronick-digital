Kronick Magazine is Back!

The World’s Greatest Free ‘Zine is now Digital and about to put a fresh “Footprint” in the Game. What began as a local Los Angeles based bi-monthly periodical in 1993 eventually became a national publication that ran until 2005.

Meshack Blaq – Publishing Editor

Meshack Blaq got his start in Hip Hop by creating the title of Boogie Down Productions/KRS1 1990 album “Edutainment = Education + Entertainment”. With some advice from Chuck-D of Public Enemy, Blaq took the royalties and started “No Sellout” Magazine in 1992 with no previous experience in Journalism nor Publishing. After learning “What not to do”, the magazine folded by the end of the year. The next year Blaq started a Public Access TV show called “Kronick Video”. It was Rapper Ice-T that encouraged Blaq to continue in Publishing by introducing him to executives at Priority Records and Eazy-E of Ruthless Records who became the 1st major advertisers of Kronick Magazine in November of 1993.

By 2005, print media was on the decline as the Internet became the newest wave of media influencers with the advent of Facebook, YouTube, Google, etc. Now in 2022, the “Rip Van Winkle” of Hip Hop has reawakened to digitize the Kronick & No Sellout Archives for licensing, create a series of NFT Trading Cards, and retell the stories of the Golden Age of Hip Hop to a new generation consisting of a global marketplace.

With exclusive Audio, Visual, and rare hard copies of magazines, Kronick Digital Media has “Hip Hop History” in a time capsule. Most of the archival footage of artists at the beginning of their careers has never been released. Many of which are still relevant over 20 years later.
